Overview

What CoopWise is

African rotating-savings cooperatives are already a trusted, working system — CoopWise doesn't reinvent them, it brings them online. The platform digitizes group savings, layers in AI to automate the repetitive parts of running a cooperative (rules, reminders, scheduling, dispute triage), and works toward blockchain-based decentralization and escrow underneath — without ever requiring members to understand or touch Web3 directly. People save money the way they always have; the infrastructure just gets more transparent and more automated over time.

Build Order

Feature Roadmap

Each feature is sequenced on top of the last — this is the order they're being built in, not just prioritized in.

0
MVP core

Group Savings

The foundational feature: members pool funds together toward a shared goal. Nothing else in CoopWise matters until this works reliably.

1
AI

Group Management & Governance

AI-driven group creation and governance — generating proposals for group rules, contribution schedules, reminders, and membership controls. Communication moves onto channels people already trust: a WhatsApp / Telegram bot interface, with the AI system plugged in behind it.

2
Wallets

Wallet Funding

Users fund wallets through Web2-native payment methods they're already familiar with. The underlying Web3 provisioning service sits behind a service-agnostic placeholder, so any provider can be swapped in without members ever needing to know Web3 exists.

3
Trust

Transparency

Every group transaction is logged and visible on the CoopWise dashboard, backed by a robust notification system from day one. On-chain event logs are introduced once escrow moves on-chain.

4
AI

Dispute Resolution

AI-driven workflows resolve contribution and payout disputes — flagged either by group members or automatically by the system when a contribution is missed. Dispute history feeds directly into each member's credit standing.

5
On-chain

Smart Contract Automation

Fully programmable, on-chain escrow — Solana first. Each group defines its own contract rules, which can also plug into liquidity pools, letting members use their cooperative standing as proof-of-stake to access external funds.

Sequencing

Rollout Phases

Web3 is the long-term engine, not the entry point. Members onboard entirely through Web2-native tools; decentralized infrastructure is phased in underneath.

PHASE 1 — NOW

Web2 Foundation

  • FastAPI backend, Next.js frontend
  • In-house KYC and onboarding
  • Join / create cooperative groups
  • Contributions via Paystack (default rail)
  • Simulated off-chain escrow
  • Robust transaction & notification logging
PHASE 2 — NEXT

Pluggable Web3 Layer

  • Modular wallet-provisioning provider (currently Crossmint, BYOA)
  • AI group management live on WhatsApp / Telegram
  • On-chain event logs introduced
  • Provider-agnostic: swappable as needs evolve
PHASE 3 — FUTURE

Decentralized Core

  • Programmable escrow on Solana
  • Per-group smart contract rules
  • Liquidity-pool integration via group standing
  • Multi-chain expansion beyond Solana
Architecture

Identity & Wallets

CoopWise owns identity end-to-end; wallet infrastructure is a separate, pluggable concern.

Identity

  • Phone OTP, email OTP, and Google via Firebase
  • No local passwords, anywhere in the system
  • African-specific KYC built in-house, not outsourced

Wallets

  • Crossmint used purely for wallet provisioning (BYOA custom tokens)
  • Provisioning runs as a background Celery task
  • Never blocks login — wallet setup happens asynchronously

Workflows

Dispute Resolution

Disputes are raised by a member or auto-flagged by the AI system when a contribution is missed, then routed through a structured resolution path — evidence, member communication, deadlines, admin escalation — before an AI-suggested resolution or, if needed, a group vote. Outcomes feed each member's credit standing over time.
